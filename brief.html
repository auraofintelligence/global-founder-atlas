Re-run the search anytime

The reusable research brief.

This is the prompt that built the atlas. It is tuned to stop a researcher returning a generic graveyard of expired startup grants, and to distinguish genuine non-dilutive support from marketing fluff. Copy it, run it in a capable research tool, verify the results at source, then fold new finds back into this same scored structure.

Conduct a comprehensive, current international search for funding, incentives, startup programs and relocation opportunities designed to attract foreign founders, ideas, technology ventures, social enterprises, creative projects and experimental research.

I am an Australian founder and multidisciplinary project creator based on North Stradbroke Island / Minjerribah, Queensland. I may remain primarily based in Australia, establish an international entity, participate temporarily overseas, form a local partnership, license intellectual property, undertake a pilot project, or relocate for part of the year where the opportunity justifies it.

Search globally, including national governments, state and city governments, special economic zones, free zones, innovation districts, sovereign wealth funds, development agencies, universities, research institutes, startup accelerators, incubators, venture studios, foundations, international organisations and corporate innovation programs.

Use initiatives such as Start Qatar as one example of the kind of opportunity being sought, but do not limit the search to Qatar or the Middle East.

My current and emerging project areas include: extended reality, spatial computing and embodied AI; personal digital twins of the body and mind; human-centred AI assistants and cognitive architecture; privacy-preserving personal data systems; generative AI, blockchain and IoT integration; health, wellbeing, ageing, dementia support and preventative health; hyperbaric oxygen research and digitally measured self-development; civic technology, participatory democracy and transparent governance; local government innovation and community infrastructure; disaster resilience and community trust-building; smart communities, digital noticeboards and public information systems; decentralised municipal AI infrastructure and distributed computing; sustainable development and regenerative community systems; Indigenous, island and remote-community innovation where partnerships are invited and culturally appropriate; film, music, immersive storytelling, festivals and creative technology; museums, cultural archives and mixed-media preservation; sports, recreation, wellbeing and public-space activation; climate adaptation, environmental monitoring and island biosecurity; education, experimental universities, research labs and citizen science; nonprofit, social-enterprise and mission-driven international collaboration; and ambitious long-term initiatives aligned with the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

Look for: non-dilutive grants and challenge prizes; founder grants, living stipends and fellowships; startup visas, entrepreneur visas and residency pathways; free or subsidised company formation; tax holidays, R&D tax incentives and special economic-zone benefits; free or subsidised office, studio, laboratory or prototyping space; cloud computing, AI compute, software and technical-service credits; university research partnerships and funded research placements; paid pilot projects with governments, councils, hospitals, universities or corporations; accelerators that accept early-stage ideas, pre-revenue ventures or solo founders; programs supporting Australian or international applicants; funding for social enterprises, nonprofits, cooperatives or hybrid structures; creative-industry, music, film, immersive-media and cultural grants; climate, civic, health, Indigenous, island and remote-community innovation funding; programs offering introductions to investors, government customers or strategic partners; land, facilities, equipment or infrastructure incentives; founder-in-residence, entrepreneur-in-residence and artist-in-residence opportunities; competitions, hackathons and open innovation calls with meaningful prizes or pilots; programs that support multiple connected ventures or venture studios; and programs expected to open soon, recurring annual programs and expressions of interest.

Prioritise opportunities where: Australians are eligible; an overseas founder can apply before establishing a local entity; the program accepts concept-stage, prototype-stage or pre-revenue projects; a solo founder or small informal team can apply; intellectual property can remain primarily owned by the founder; relocation is optional, temporary or supported financially; the program offers substantial practical value rather than merely mentoring; applications are currently open, officially announced, recurring or likely to reopen; English-language applications are accepted; the program is compatible with unconventional, interdisciplinary or long-horizon ideas; the opportunity could support a pilot on Minjerribah, elsewhere in Australia, or in the host country; and there is potential to combine the program with Australian grants or international partnerships.

For every opportunity, provide: program name; country and city or region; official organisation; official source link; current application status; opening and closing dates using exact dates; whether the program is recurring; applicant eligibility; whether Australian citizens and entities can apply; whether local incorporation, residency or relocation is required; stage of development accepted; industries or themes supported; funding amount and currency; whether the funding is a grant, investment, loan, prize, reimbursement, tax incentive or in-kind support; equity requested if any; other benefits such as visas, housing, workspace, laboratory access, compute credits or introductions; intellectual-property conditions; matching-fund or co-investment requirements; key application documents; estimated application difficulty; which of my project areas it most closely matches; a concise explanation of why it may be relevant; any material risks, restrictions or hidden trade-offs; and a confidence rating based on the quality and currency of the official information.

Separate the results into: A. Applications currently open; B. Announced opportunities opening within the next twelve months; C. Strong recurring programs whose present round is closed; D. Startup relocation and foreign-founder attraction packages; E. Research, university and laboratory opportunities; F. Government and corporate paid-pilot opportunities; G. Social-impact, nonprofit and sustainable-development opportunities; H. Creative, cultural, music, film and immersive-media opportunities; I. Highly ambitious or unusual wildcard opportunities.

Rank the strongest opportunities using a weighted score: eligibility for an Australian founder 20%; non-dilutive financial value 15%; alignment with my projects 20%; openness to early-stage ideas 10%; practical infrastructure and partnership value 10%; founder-friendly intellectual-property terms 10%; low relocation or incorporation burden 5%; likelihood of a credible application 10%.

Begin with an executive shortlist of the 15 strongest opportunities. For each, recommend the best project or combination of projects to present, the most persuasive framing, and the next concrete application step. Then identify: the five easiest credible applications; the five largest potential funding opportunities; the five strongest relocation or international-expansion packages; the five best opportunities for Aura of Intelligence; the five best civic or community-technology opportunities; the five best health, longevity or dementia-related opportunities; the five best creative and immersive-media opportunities; the five best opportunities for G.A.J.R.A. Earth or a nonprofit structure; and any opportunities that could fund an on-island pilot or research facility on Minjerribah.

Search in English and, where useful, use translated local-language search terms. Verify important details from official program or government sources rather than relying solely on blogs, social media posts or funding databases. Exclude expired one-off programs unless they provide evidence of a likely future round. Clearly label anything uncertain, outdated or based on secondary reporting. Do not invent deadlines, eligibility or funding values.

Finish with a practical 90-day funding-search and application strategy that groups compatible opportunities, identifies reusable application materials, and avoids chasing low-value programs that require disproportionate paperwork.

For a faster first pass, return no more than 30 carefully verified opportunities rather than a large unfiltered list.

Per-opportunity field spec

Every card in the atlas captures these fields, so a new find drops straight into the same structure.

Program name
Official title of the program
Country / city
Where it operates and where you would need to be
Official organisation
The body that runs and funds it
Source link
The official page, not a blog or database
Status and dates
Open, recurring or soon, with exact dates where confirmed
Recurring
Whether it runs again on a cycle
Eligibility and AU access
Who can apply, and whether Australians and Australian entities qualify
Local entity / relocation
Whether incorporation, residency or relocation is required
Stage accepted
Concept, prototype, pre-revenue or growth
Themes supported
The industries and topics it funds
Funding amount and type
Value and currency, and whether grant, investment, loan, prize, reimbursement, tax incentive or in-kind
Equity requested
Any equity taken
Other benefits
Visas, housing, workspace, lab access, compute credits, introductions
IP conditions
What you keep and any open-source or access terms
Matching / co-funding
Any co-investment required
Key documents
What an application needs
Difficulty
Estimated effort to apply credibly
Project match and why
Which of your areas it fits and the reason it is relevant
Risks
Material restrictions or hidden trade-offs
Confidence and verify
How solid the current information is, and what to re-check at source
Weighted score and pitch
The 0 to 100 score, plus best project, framing and next step
